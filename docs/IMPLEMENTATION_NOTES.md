# Implementation Notes

## Image-Based Lighting

Image-Based Lighting uses an omnidirectional HDR image as a measurement of incoming real-world radiance. The renderer places synthetic objects inside this captured lighting environment and samples the environment during shading instead of relying on only hand-placed lights.

The supplied Debevec references motivate the same idea: capture or load high dynamic range incident illumination, map it onto an environment, and use that measured light to illuminate synthetic geometry.

## Runtime Pipeline

- `src/loaders/hdr.ts` decodes Radiance RGBE `.hdr` files into linear RGB values and uploads them as `rgba16float`.
- `src/ibl/processor.ts` runs all preprocessing render passes.
- `src/shaders/iblPreprocess.ts` contains the equirectangular mapping, irradiance convolution, GGX importance sampling, and BRDF LUT integration WGSL.
- `src/shaders/renderShaders.ts` contains the PBR and skybox WGSL.
- `src/renderer/renderer.ts` binds the precomputed IBL textures and draws the scene and comparison modes.

## Diffuse Irradiance

Diffuse IBL uses a low-frequency cubemap generated by hemisphere convolution. For each output normal direction, the shader integrates incoming environment radiance weighted by `cos(theta)`. This approximates Lambertian irradiance and makes rough diffuse surfaces respond to the whole environment, not only direct reflections.

## Specular Prefiltering

Specular IBL cannot sample the raw environment directly for rough materials because rough reflection covers a cone of directions. The prefilter pass stores increasingly blurred GGX importance-sampled environment maps in mip levels. The PBR shader samples the mip level corresponding to material roughness.

## BRDF LUT

The split-sum approximation separates the specular IBL integral into:

- a prefiltered environment lookup dependent on reflection direction and roughness,
- a 2D BRDF integration LUT dependent on `NdotV` and roughness.

The LUT stores the scale/bias terms used with Fresnel-Schlick in the final PBR shader.

## PBR Equations

The final material shader implements:

- Fresnel-Schlick: `fresnelSchlick`
- GGX/Trowbridge-Reitz NDF: `distributionGGX`
- Smith geometry term: `geometrySmith`
- Diffuse irradiance: `textureSampleLevel(irradianceMap, ...)`
- Split-sum specular: `prefilterMap * (F * brdf.x + brdf.y)`
- Tone mapping and gamma correction: `tonemap`

All HDR/environment calculations are linear until final tone mapping and gamma correction.

## Validation

- The skybox should be upright after equirectangular-to-cubemap conversion.
- The mirror sphere uses `reflect(-V, N)` in the PBR shader.
- A chrome sphere can show warped or vertically inverted reflections because it behaves like a curved mirror.
- Changing roughness should change reflection sharpness.
- Changing the HDR environment should change both lighting and reflections.
- The roughness strip should show sharper reflections on low roughness and softer reflections on high roughness.
- The environment comparison should change both lighting and reflection color.
